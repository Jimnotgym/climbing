What is this site???

My local climbing guide is out of print. This means climbing is restricted to the old guard and the people who can track down a copy of the old guide. There is a clique, as in all hobbies, which don't seem to mind. I hear nationally that x guidebook has been 5 years in the making. For all of that 5 years the information is only available to the 'in-crowd'. After that they rely on some group putting up the cash to publish the guide, or all of the volunteers work will be for zero. The commercial realities, and it seems the local politics mean that lots of climbing areas do not have a current guidebook. Meanwhile some local groups write their own guides and have to put up their own website. These tend to whither over time, as the overheads of maintaining an online presence, and trying to present and update guidebooks tends to make them age less than gracefully.

But these are problems that have been solved! The internet, Google, Amazon and Facebook are built on Free and Open Source Software, like GNU/Linux, Apache, MySQL and PHP or the Python and Jinja2 that rendered this site. This is software that has been collaberatively built by tens-of-thousands of maintainers and freed from the burden of copyright (or actually using copyright laws to enforce freedom) it is easy for people to make derivative works or pick up and maintain an abandoned project. This means the information is free to spread and live forever.

Over time this movement has built amazing collaberation tools, and it is our intention to leverage those tools to build a guide that will stay up to date and will never go out of print, by crowdsourcing the work. We are also publishing under the Creative Commons Attribution ShareAlike License like Wikipedia does. You can see the full details in the footer of this page, but basically it means that you are free to copy print and re-use this work as long as you attribute it to this project (called 'Open Climbing' as a working title) and re-share it under the same terms as we gave it to you. This means a guidebook company could copy verbatim our route descriptions but would have to say where they got them, and allow people to copy them and any derivative works from also under the same license.

Joining in

You can find this full project on Github. From there if you are tech savvy you could contribute content directly like developers do. Look up how to 'fork' and 'pull request' as this is the best way to contribute both to the site and the guidebook content. More help coming soon with how to do this. However if you have route descriptions or even a complete guidebook you would like us to look after please do get in touch as we would gladly open-source it for you, so that it may be maintained forever by the community.
Please be quite certain that you own the copyright to the material you are handing to us. Route names are not subject to copyright (in our opinion) any more than street names are. Otherwise you could never tell anyone you had climed Dreams of White Horses without paying a license to the person that named it! Route descriptions and access notes are however subject to copyright, so please don't copy them from guidebooks. Any authors who do use this site must accept that by giving us the information they are expressley giving permission to have it published under the Creative Commons License. You can contact us at the Github link above where you will find issue trackers to report bugs and any problems.
